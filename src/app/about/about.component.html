ClaimsKG Statistical Observatory

Overview

Claims KG Statistics Tool is an advanced extension of the Claims KG project, designed to provide comprehensive statistical insights and graphical representations of fact-checking articles stored in the Claims KG knowledge graph. This tool enables users to dive deeper into the data, gaining valuable insights into the publishing trends, sources, languages, topics, and other significant attributes of the collected fact-checking articles.

Just like the ClaimsKG search engine, the statistical observatory is the result of a collaborative effort between French and German research teams. It is made available to the public for research purposes, while respecting the copyrights of the source fact-checking websites.

What is Claims KG?

Claims KG is a project dedicated to aggregating articles from a variety of trustworthy websites, creating a robust knowledge graph. This graph is enriched through sophisticated algorithms that extract additional knowledge, providing a rich and structured dataset. The primary goal of Claims KG is to enhance information credibility and accessibility, contributing to the fight against misinformation by relying on reputable sources.

This statistical observatory allows for the visual exploration of various metrics and trends related to claims and their verification across the different fact-checking websites included in ClaimsKG.

Key Definitions

  • Entities: entites are physical unites that are ment to descirble a location, a person an organisations an event, etc..
  • Truth rating: This criteria refers to the credibility given to the claims according to the website from where it got extracted. Every website has its own ranking but in this interface only 4 types of truth ratings are possible (taken from the normalised rating scheme of ClaimsKG): TRUE, FALSE, MIXTURE, OTHER. Only labeled a claim as TRUE or FALSE if it was completely true or completely false and did not have any ambiguity in their ratings. MIXTURE is assigned to claims which hold a degree of both truth and falsehood, such as “half-true”, or “misleading”. OTHER is for claims that do not fit into the TRUE/FALSE or MIXTURE categories and has rating names like “Pending Investigation” or “photo out of context”, among several others.
  • Time period: You can select a start date and an end date which will define the time period of the claims you're looking for.
  • Topics: The topics are generated autoumatically from the claims kg plateform and are only available for the claims/articles that are written in english. So the search engines (topic / topic-entity) are only for english claims
  • Sources: The sources are the websites were the articles/claims were extracted from (ex. AFP Fact Check).
  • Keywords: Keywords are automatically extracted from claims(articles). For example, a claim concerning COVID-19 may be tagged with "Economy".

Key Features:

  • Visualization of the total number of claims and fact-checking reviews over time
  • Distribution of truth evaluations (true, false, mixte, others)
  • Analysis of the frequency of appearance of different entities (people, organizations, locations, etc.) associated with the claims
  • Evolution of the number of claims by thematic category (politics, health, economy, etc.)
  • Comparison of the performance of different fact-checking websites in terms of coverage volume and results
